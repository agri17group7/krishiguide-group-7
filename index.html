Question

Which cucurbit suits coastal Khulna?

A farmer in coastal Khulna wants to grow cucurbits. Which crop would you suggest among cucumber, ridge gourd, snake gourd, and pointed gourd—and why?

Open →
Field context: saline-prone soil • occasional waterlogging • humid tropics
Answer

Best pick: SNAKE GOURD (Trichosanthes cucumerina)

✅ Why Snake Gourd?

  1. Better Tolerance to Salinity & Waterlogging: Snake gourd performs better than cucumber and pointed gourd under moderate salinity (common in coastal Khulna). It also withstands temporary waterlogging during the rainy season, unlike cucumber which is highly sensitive.
  2. Lower Disease Pressure: Cucumber is very prone to downy mildew, mosaic virus, and fruit fly in coastal humid climates. Pointed gourd is vulnerable to wilt and requires vegetative propagation. Snake gourd is comparatively hardier with fewer major disease outbreaks.
  3. Climatic Suitability: The humid and warm climate of coastal Khulna is ideal for snake gourd growth and flowering. Pointed gourd prefers fertile loamy soils with low salinity, which is less common in Khulna.
  4. Market Value & Demand: Snake gourd has steady demand in local and urban markets. Farmers can harvest fruits continuously over a long period, ensuring regular income.

👉 Final Suggestion

Among cucumber, snake gourd, and pointed gourd, the best choice for coastal Khulna is snake gourd due to its adaptability to saline soils, tolerance to waterlogging, and lower pest/disease susceptibility compared to cucumber and pointed gourd.
